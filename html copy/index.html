Learn More About Lemon

Lemon, is an educational kit for introductory modeling, mechanics, electronics, and programming courses, that enables learners to engage with all these concepts while enhancing their computational and design thinking skills. It consists of 3 consecutive robots that each represent a level of learning and a stage of mechanical complexity in organisms. Each stage teaches the learner how to make a biomimetic robot and it advances in difficulty with each stage, so the learners get a chance to experience all three stages, fully comprehending all the concepts necessary to make a functional robot. The simplest stage is the fish robot which is the first step of the kit, the next is a turtle, and the third is a dog. The kit consists of laser-cut kraftplex, screws, Raspberry Pi Zero, servos, motor drivers, cables, a battery holder, batteries, breadboard, and an ultrasonic sensor. It is a kit that allows learners to experience and learn about mechanics, coding, robotics, and electronics based on the workings of organisms and the help of the instructional manual of the kit. Lemon kits are not only prototyping kits but also STEM kits. When completing every stage, makers will be able to do the mathematics and kinematics calculations. Ever since the COVID-19 pandemic, many people are unaware of the meaning, logic, and purpose of FabLabs (Fabrication Laboratories). Therefore, our aim is to spread the culture of the FabLabs (Fabrication Laboratories) community with Lemon kits. Firstly starting with our community, we will introduce the kits to the newcomer students of the FabLab in our school.



Technicalities: Robotics and Education


Technology is ubiquitous and integrated into every aspect of our lives. This newly accelerating integration of technology in our lives is causing a rapid change in our traditional way of living. Although the world is rapidly changing, public education has maintained almost the same system since its introduction to the world. Though educational reform efforts have been made around the world, the trouble lies in the fact that the majority of schools are trying to prepare students for the future by continuing what was done in the past. In the last few years, there was a prodigious increase in the implementation of robotics in our lifestyles and yet the adaptation required in the education systems around the globe was not as effective as is necessary. An example of these inefficient integrations is the STEM curriculums presented in schools, also known as the “Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics” curriculums however these usually do not include an elaborative comprehension of robotics as a whole. As robots are a combination of hardware and software, and the two concepts are closely related, it is exceptionally complicated for beginners to fully understand either one of these subjects in the absence of the other, making it extremely challenging for beginners to build robots, and students to learn about in simple courses about electronics or mechanics.

Our proposed educational kit Lemon is aiming to teach all beginners in robotics how to design, assemble and program robots, using laser cutting, Raspberry Pi Zero, servos, and thonny. The kit consists of 3 models that were designed with respect to the stages of evolution in the nature. In each stage new and advanced abilities are added to the model resulting in the acquirement of new knowledge by the learner regarding mechanics, electronics, design, and/or programming. The learners get to experience using new systems and abilities by testing them in each subsequent model and in each stage, a new robot that resembles a more advanced organism in the animal kingdom is built by the learner.

The educational kit Lemon is designed to have 3 models that range from the simplest to the most advanced. The first model is to be the simplest, with the least amount of motors and no sensors, it will serve as an introductory robotics model for beginners. The next model will be a more active robot outcome and have certain sensors integrated. Third and the final model will be the most advanced version of the robot. This model will have multiple sensors and more complex circuits to challenge the learners and to attract their further attention.






3 Subsequent Models

Lime: Introductory Pack


“Lime” is the first and the most straightforward level of the educational kit Lemon. It requires 2 servo motors and simple circuit elements that allows the model to perform the most simple and basic movements of a fish. It is designed to provide beginners with a sample of how laser-cut pieces are assembled and how simple movements of motors are coded. The reason behind the first step of the way resembling a fish is because of the evolutionary history of the animal kingdom. Most life forms evolved initially in marine habitats therefore we chose a fish to be the initial model in our kit. In this model, the electronics are outside the robot since the fish is too small to integrate all the hardware inside of it. It is the simplest model available in the kit and a useful tool to fully comprehend how the motors work and see how the electronics work. The breadboard and the circuits are outside the model and are visible to the eye which will help the learners gain familiarity with it and understand the basics of robotics.






Satsuma: Getting Advanced


“Satsuma” is the second stage proposed in the kit. It is the biomimicry of a turtle and is more active compared to “Lime”. It consists of 4 servo motors and requires a more in-depth understanding of programming and mechanics. In this model, the electronics are hidden inside the body and the building process requires the ability to control multiple motors at once. This situation allows the learner to experiment with the given tools.






Lemon: Last Level


“Lemon” is the most advanced level available in the kit. It is the third level and requires the most advanced comprehension of all the aforementioned topics. It consists of 8 servo motors and 4 legs with femur and tibias. This model is capable of further movements than just stable consistent moves. It can walk, jump, recognize obstacles on its way and avoid them while walking. It resembles a dog in its features and the design of this model was inspired by the Stanford Quadruped.
